A Day in Sarah's Life

Sarah starts her day at 6:30 AM reviewing overnight quality alerts from global suppliers. She manages supplier quality programs across three continents for a $2B medical device manufacturer.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Oversees supplier quality programs and audit compliance
  • Manages regulatory readiness across multiple jurisdictions
  • Leads quality crisis response and incident containment
  • Reports quality metrics and ROI to executive leadership
  • Drives continuous improvement and process harmonization

Technology Stack:

Regular user of QMS platforms (MasterControl, Sparta), ERP systems (SAP), and actively evaluating cloud-based supplier quality solutions like STEPQ.

Critical Pain Points

🔥 Bandwidth Crisis: Limited internal resources for global deployments, quality events, and supplier network expansion.
🔥 Harmonization Complexity: Struggling to standardize processes across multiple sites with legacy systems.
🔥 Demonstrating Value: Difficulty quantifying quality's financial impact and pressure to show ROI.
🔥 Technology Gaps: Legacy systems lack real-time visibility and predictive insights.
🔥 Personal Accountability: High stress from personal liability and 24/7 crisis responsibility.

Goals & Success Metrics

Short-Term Goals (Current Year)

✓ Zero Critical Findings: Achieve zero critical audit findings across all facilities.
✓ 30% Reduction: Reduce supplier non-conformance incidents by 30%.
✓ 3:1 ROI: Demonstrate 3:1 ROI on quality technology investments.

Key Performance Indicators

  • Audit readiness/compliance metrics
  • Supplier defect rates (PPM)
  • Cost of poor quality (COPQ)
  • Incident resolution time & severity

Buying Process & Decision Factors

Key Buying Factors

🎯 Compliance Assurance: Solution must ensure regulatory readiness (FDA, EASA, ISO).
🎯 Proven Industry Expertise: Vendor must show deep knowledge of regulated industries.
🎯 Integration Capability: Seamless connection with existing ERP/QMS is a must.
🎯 Measurable ROI: Clear financial impact must be demonstrated within 12-18 months.

Decision Process

Sarah leads a committee-based evaluation (Quality, Supply Chain, IT, Finance) with a 6-12 month timeline, typically involving an RFP/RFI process and pilot requirements.

Common Objections to Address

⚠️ Budget Concerns: Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) benefits need to be clearly shown.
⚠️ Security & Compliance: Must prove cloud solutions meet strict data security standards.
⚠️ Change Disruption: Fear of disrupting established workflows and supplier relationships.
⚠️ Managed Service Skepticism: Uncertainty about relying on external teams for critical functions.
⚠️ IT Integration: IT department resistance to new platform integration and support.

🎯 Sales Engagement Strategy

1
Lead with Compliance & Risk: Focus on audit readiness first, then cost savings.
2
Engage Stakeholders Early: Involve IT, Finance, and Operations from the start.
3
Provide Peer References: Share case studies from similar regulated industries.
4
Offer Pilot Programs: Reduce perceived risk with flexible pilot options to validate ROI.
5
Demonstrate Industry Knowledge: Speak her language (FDA 21 CFR, ISO 13485, COPQ).
