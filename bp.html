A Day in Sarah's Life

Sarah starts her day at 6:30 AM reviewing overnight quality alerts from global suppliers. She manages supplier quality programs across three continents for a $2B medical device manufacturer.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Oversees supplier quality programs and audit compliance
  • Manages regulatory readiness across multiple jurisdictions
  • Leads quality crisis response and incident containment
  • Reports quality metrics and ROI to executive leadership
  • Drives continuous improvement and process harmonization

Technology Stack:

Regular user of QMS platforms (MasterControl, Sparta), ERP systems (SAP), and actively evaluating cloud-based supplier quality solutions like STEPQ.

Critical Pain Points

🔥 Bandwidth Crisis: Limited internal resources to handle global deployments, urgent quality events, and expanding supplier networks simultaneously.
🔥 Harmonization Complexity: Struggling to standardize processes across multiple sites, each with legacy systems and local compliance requirements.
🔥 Demonstrating Value: Difficulty quantifying quality's financial impact beyond cost avoidance - pressure to show ROI.
🔥 Technology Gaps: Legacy systems don't provide real-time visibility or predictive insights needed for proactive management.
🔥 Personal Accountability: High stress from personal liability for quality failures, 24/7 responsibility during crises.

Goals & Success Metrics

Short-Term Goals (Current Year)

✓ Zero Critical Findings: Achieve zero critical audit findings across all facilities.
✓ 30% Reduction: Reduce supplier non-conformance incidents by 30%.
✓ 3:1 ROI: Demonstrate 3:1 ROI on quality technology investments.

Key Performance Indicators

  • Audit readiness/compliance metrics
  • Supplier defect rates (PPM)
  • Cost of poor quality (COPQ)
  • Incident resolution time
  • Number and severity of regulatory findings

Buying Process & Decision Factors

Key Buying Factors

🎯 Compliance Assurance: Solution must ensure regulatory readiness across all jurisdictions (FDA, EASA, ISO).
🎯 Proven Industry Expertise: Vendor must demonstrate deep knowledge of regulated industries and similar implementations.
🎯 Integration Capability: Seamless connection with existing ERP and QMS systems is non-negotiable.
🎯 Measurable ROI: Clear financial impact must be demonstrated within 12-18 months.

Decision Process

Sarah leads a committee-based evaluation involving Quality, Supply Chain, IT, and Finance. Typical timeline: 6-12 months with RFP/RFI process and pilot requirements.

Common Objections to Address

⚠️ Budget Concerns: Worried about ongoing service costs vs. one-time software purchase. Need to show total cost of ownership benefits.
⚠️ Security & Compliance: Concerns about cloud-based solutions meeting data security and regulatory requirements.
⚠️ Change Disruption: Fear of disrupting established supplier relationships and internal processes.
⚠️ Managed Service Skepticism: Uncertainty about relying on external teams for critical quality functions.
⚠️ IT Integration: IT department resistance to new platform integration and support requirements.
